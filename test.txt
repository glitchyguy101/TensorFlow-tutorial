It’s an allegory for maintaining one's self-worth and resilience when placed in any seemingly hopeless position—a bad job, a failed relationship, or an actual prison.

The film's flawless structure, its emotional depth, and its immensely satisfying ending deliver a profound spiritual catharsis. It transcends the "prison drama" label to become a cinematic affirmation of faith, friendship, and the unquenchable human desire for freedom. It is, simply put, a perfect motion picture.
